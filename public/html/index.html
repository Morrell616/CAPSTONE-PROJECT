Crochet is a technique of creating fabric by interlocking loops of yarn or thread using a hook. The exact origins of are a bit of a mystery, but its believed to have roots in the early 19th-century Europe. In the 19th century, crochet gained widespread recognition, especially during the Victorian era. As the craft evolved, s did the variety of stitches and patterns, leading to the creation of lacework, garments, and household items.
